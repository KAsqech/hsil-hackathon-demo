What is the HSIL Annual Hackathon?

A catalyst for practical innovation that empowers participants to propose and build solutions improving healthcare delivery and system performance. The event nurtures unconventional thinking and bridges the gap between theory and practice—so solutions can translate into real‑world change.

Who joins?

Students, clinicians, engineers, designers, policy analysts, entrepreneurs, and operators—forming interdisciplinary teams at Harvard and partner hubs worldwide.

Focus Areas

Each year’s theme centers on building high‑value health systems, with emphasis on digital health and responsible AI. Detailed challenge statements are shared in the Primer and at kickoff.

System Resilience

  • Governance & financing visibility
  • Resource allocation & surge capacity
  • Preparedness dashboards

Equity & Public Health

  • Access & outcome gap‑closing tools
  • Community signal & trust platforms
  • Bias monitoring & evaluation

Data → Outcomes

  • Primary care capture & visualization
  • Care‑gap closure & referral loops
  • Outcome tracking & feedback

How it works

Day 1 — Kickoff

  • Keynote & challenge deep‑dives
  • Team formation & problem framing
  • Mentor office hours

Day 2 — Build

  • Workshops: clinical, product, policy
  • Data labs & testing
  • Midpoint reviews

Day 3 — Demo Day

  • Pitch polishing
  • Judging & showcases
  • Awards & next steps
